I've been with the same company (Ocean Infinity) for 5 years, working on several projects as seen in my curriculum, mainly in computer vision applied to underwater data (sonar, cameras, point clouds).
My biggest project was the auto-annotation product for object detection in side-scan sonar — I steered it from the beginning, took it to product, and it cut annotation time from weeks to hours.
I also really enjoyed projects like the Bayesian target search using sonar and the real-time alignment aid tool (segmenting underwater drill bit components). I was Product Owner on those two — handling quarterly planning, stakeholder engagement, and task distribution — which let me grow into the management side as well as the technical side.
Other Ocean Infinity work I'm proud of: an LLM-based classifier for maritime Daily Progress Reports that assigns 17 operational categories to time-stamped event logs via structured-output prompting (LangChain + OpenAI), replacing a manual report-compilation process; multi-beam sonar survey alignment with GPU-accelerated optimisation for xyz positioning (cutting weeks down to hours); point cloud noise reduction and cleaning with PointNet and Cube (days to hours); inspection segmentation/detection achieving over 80% F1 with complementary OCR (tesseract) for asset identification; autonomous pipeline following for ROVs, fusing magnetometer and navigation data with Kalman filtering to replace a manual joystick operator; research into monocular and stereo SLAM with NeRFs and Gaussian Splatting, integrated in C++ via ROS2 and FoxGlove; and classification/eventing of underwater assets in video streams using CNNs and Transformers.
